Who We Are

Founded in 2016, AfriHUB is a capacity building/strengthening local organization that connects the missing dots building local organization's and Institution's capacity and to link them to the global community to foster sustained development efforts in Nigeria. The organization came to being by a genius woman, a visionary, strategic think tank and a goal getter who started work in the public sector as a junior staff at the age of 20 years and later grew to a senior officer after attending several schools, growth and development courses within and outside Nigeria (self-sponsored). After working for 18 years in the public sector decided to move on to gather more experience in the development work where she gathered lots of experience to set up AfriHUB. Brought up in a very poor family that cannot afford a dollar per day, subjected domestic violence, child abuse, molestation, bad treatment and denial of right to school. The horrible upbringing triggered the need to fight for the right of children, girls, women, and the vulnerable groups and to empower them to have a voice or say, to also support develop people skills to earn their living. Today, over 300 youths were empowered through the AfriHUB, women agency increased, girls and boys from poor background, orphans, those with disabilities and less privilege developed self-confidence, resilience, self- reliance and empowered to seek information, make informed choices and take decision and be responsible for their actions. Youth are trained and mentored on resilience, adaptability and determination to improve their lives by AfriHUB.


What We Do

We build local capacities, protect the right of those who have no voice or voiceless and empower women and youth to gain their agency and right to their own life and be self-reliance, adapt to situations in life and be productive regardless of their past experiences. Initiatives includes building Community Resilience on Disaster Management to be able to prepare, respond and mange emergencies. AfriHUB have Human Rights Department with five (5) legal personnel working voluntarily to defend the rights of those whose rights infringed. The team comprised of men and women lawyers who work in collaboration with the National Bar Association (Human right section). They offer legal support regardless of race, color, gender orientation and does not discriminate. We empower women and youth through skills enhancement and acquisition programs so that they become creative and earn their living in a sustainable way without relying on others. We provide education materials, fees and psychosocial support to less privilege children, those with disability and orphans.


Who do we work with

  • Partnership: We collaborate with other stakeholders in each of our thematic area. We built partnerships with organizations of like minds for diverse purposes and has led the formation of Consortium, Cluster, Coalition, Networks and Umbrella Organizations and provide technical assistants for their functionality. We also network with Academia and , with Researchers.
  • Government: Bearing sustainability in mind, we make sure State government and local government are carried along right from planning stages to the end of all our interventions. We allow them to take ownership and drive the process in principles. We engage the local government authorities in the communities where we work, and they cooperate with us. The relationship management has always been fruitful and productive.
  • Communities: We mobilize local communities through community structures and individuals who have volunteer to commit their time and little resources for development. We plan with their representatives to get their pulse in whatever we do.


Our Mission

Devoted to enhancing effectiveness of local organizations and provision of humanitarian services for and sustainable development.

Our Goal

Empower and boost local capacity towards sustainable development in line with the SDGs goal and provision of humanitarian services with protection of human and children rights.

Our Vision

Connecting the Dots by linking the local communities in Africa to the Global communities for sustained and effective economic growth and development.

Accountability & Transparency

AfriHUB Staff should take responsible for actions and decisions and have the ability to provide sound reasoning for their actions. Activities are to be conducted in an open and clear manner.

Respect for Persons

Respect for oneself and for others to be demonstrated through good manners, courtesy, politeness, and a general awareness of their rights, wishes, concerns, and feelings.

Equal Right

Staff to be treated fairly and be given equal opportunity and rights respected regardless of rank, position, racial difference or color of skin, marital status, tribe or religion, learned or illiterate. Persons with disability should be treated with fairness without discrimination.

Integrity

Staff are expected to behave ethically right in the workplace and outside the workplace for good reputation of the organization. Every staff should embrace politeness of mannerism and courtesy in behaviour or speech, treat each other with civility and respect.

Honesty

Staff should be known for telling the truth, being upright, candid and sincere with what you say and do.

Responsive Speed

Staff should be known for their proactiveness, acting fast/quick to respond to task or any given assignment producing timely quality results.
